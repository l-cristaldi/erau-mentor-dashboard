Meeting Blueprints

The Five Phases of Mentoring: B-E-P-A-C

  • Belief: Cultivate steadfast belief through high expectations and encouragement
  • Effort: Inspire extraordinary effort and commitment to excellence
  • Progression: Facilitate consistent forward movement and momentum
  • Accountability: Encourage responsibility and ownership of outcomes
  • Confidence: Instill self-assurance through achievements and growth

Initial Meeting (60 minutes)

Pre-Meeting Preparation

Opening & Core Discussion

Sample Conversation Starters:
  • "Welcome! I'm excited to be your mentor. Tell me, what drew you to ERAU?"
  • "What are you most excited about this semester?"
  • "What do you hope to get out of our mentorship?"
Essential Questions:
Academic:
  • "How are you feeling about your course load?"
  • "What academic areas do you want to strengthen?"
  • "What study strategies have worked for you?"
Career & Goals:
  • "What career path interests you most?"
  • "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
  • "What skills do you want to develop?"

Closing

Regular Follow-up Meetings (45 minutes)

Check-in & Review

Check-in Questions:
  • "How has your week been going overall?"
  • "Let's check in on those action items from last time..."
  • "What's been on your mind since we last met?"

Planning & Action

Closing Scripts:
  • "So our action items for this week are..."
  • "I'm confident you can tackle these challenges because..."
  • "Remember, I'm here if you need support before our next meeting."

Mid-Semester Check-in (60 minutes)

Review Questions:
  • "How do you feel about your academic progress so far?"
  • "Which goals have you made the most progress on?"
  • "What achievements are you most proud of?"
  • "What patterns do you notice in your challenges?"

Final Transition Meeting (60 minutes)

Reflection Prompts:
  • "When you think back to our first meeting, how have you grown?"
  • "What are you most proud of accomplishing?"
  • "What challenges did you overcome that surprised you?"
  • "What skills have you developed through our work together?"
Future Planning Questions:
  • "What are your goals for the next semester/year?"
  • "How will you maintain the momentum you've built?"
  • "Who else can support you in your continued growth?"
  • "What resources will be most valuable to you going forward?"

The Five Phases of Mentoring: B-E-P-A-C

The underlying principles forming the basis of these five phases were developed over decades of teaching and mentoring students in a wide range of high school and college settings. This approach has helped countless students unlock their academic potential and pave the way for a successful future.

  • Belief: Cultivate steadfast belief through high expectations and encouragement. Apply the Pygmalion Effect - high expectations create self-fulfilling prophecies.
  • Effort: Inspire extraordinary effort and commitment to excellence. Emphasize that there are no shortcuts to success - "old-fashioned hard work" is central to achievement.
  • Progression: Facilitate consistent forward movement and momentum building. Help students see everything as progression: every assignment, course, semester.
  • Accountability: Encourage responsibility and ownership of outcomes. Help mentees examine setbacks objectively and learn from mistakes to avoid patterns of poor performance.
  • Confidence: Instill self-assurance through achievements and growth recognition. Build confidence through utilizing natural talents AND overcoming challenges.

Key Principles for Each Phase

Belief - Cultivating High Expectations:
  • "If you raise the bar, they will find a way over"
  • Instill unshakeable belief that mentees can achieve ambitious objectives
  • Create self-fulfilling prophecies through positive expectations
  • Make certain mentees truly believe their aspirations can become reality
Effort - Inspiring Hard Work:
  • "Belief is the catalyst, effort brings vision into focus"
  • Promote daily routines that prioritize effort above the norm
  • There is no substitute for doing the work required, day after day
  • Muhammad Ali: "Suffer now and live the rest of your life as a champion"
Progression - Building Momentum:
  • "Momentum is the fuel that leads to sustained success"
  • Stack one success on top of another in daily pursuit of excellence
  • Encourage effective time management and strategic planning
  • Success breeds success - winning becomes a habit
Accountability - Learning from Setbacks:
  • Focus on quick course correction followed by winning streaks
  • Setbacks are inevitable - examine why things went wrong
  • Michael Jordan: "I've failed over and over again, and that is why I succeed"
  • Promote resilient attitudes when stumbling blocks are encountered
Confidence - Building Self-Assurance:
  • "Confidence germinates from the seeds of belief"
  • Craft customized strategies to unlock individual potential
  • Confidence comes from thriving at strengths AND overcoming weaknesses
  • Prepare students to enter careers with confidence and leadership capability

The Three Pillars of Effective Mentoring

The foundation of successful mentoring relationships built on trust, growth, and empowerment. These pillars support the B-E-P-A-C framework and guide every interaction with mentees.

1. Build Academic Confidence

  • Foundation: Help mentees concentrate on what they can do instead of what they can't do
  • Approach: Confidence comes from thriving at strengths AND performing beyond expectations in challenging areas
  • Goal: Build unshakeable belief that mentees can and will succeed
Questions to Ask Mentees:
  • When it comes to school, which courses/subjects do you tend to naturally excel in?
  • Conversely, which courses/subjects do you tend to struggle with?
  • Thinking about your current schedule, which classes fall into your "strength" category vs. "challenge" category?
  • What is your specific plan to overcome the anticipated challenges in difficult subjects?
  • How do you typically respond when you don't perform as well as expected?
  • What does academic success look like to you this semester?
Mentor Reflection Questions:
  • How do I help mentees identify their natural talents and strengths?
  • What strategies do I use to help them overcome challenging subjects?
  • Am I encouraging high expectations while providing adequate support?

2. Achieve Maximum Potential

  • Foundation: Excellence is a habit formed through repetition and strategic time management
  • Approach: Make sound academic habits as natural as daily routines
  • Goal: Help mentees become accustomed to winning and doing things that make winning possible
Questions to Ask Mentees:
  • What is your favorite time of day? When are you most productive?
  • Are you a morning person or a night person, and how does this impact your daily routine?
  • What blocks of time do you need to account for on a daily basis?
  • What is the best way to arrange these times so that you maximize your productivity?
  • What are the primary "time wasters" in your life, and what are you going to do to eliminate them?
  • What good habits do you want to make a part of your everyday routine?
  • How do you currently handle procrastination?
Mentor Reflection Questions:
  • How do I help mentees identify optimal study and work times?
  • What strategies do I use to help them eliminate procrastination and time wasters?
  • Am I helping them understand that momentum leads to sustained success?

3. Prepare for Future Success

  • Foundation: Strategic planning and long-term vision over immediate gratification
  • Approach: Everything is a progression - every assignment, course, semester
  • Goal: Help mentees see how current actions connect to future opportunities
Questions to Ask Mentees:
  • What do you see yourself doing in five years? How about in ten years?
  • What must you accomplish between now and then in order to make this vision a reality?
  • What is the biggest obstacle to achieving your vision, and how do you plan to overcome this obstacle?
  • What are you doing right now at ERAU to actively work towards achieving this vision?
  • How do you balance immediate desires with long-term goals?
  • What opportunities are you currently pursuing or considering?
  • What would you regret not pursuing while you're at ERAU?
Mentor Reflection Questions:
  • How do I help mentees develop clear 5-year and 10-year visions?
  • What strategies do I use to help them connect current academic work to future goals?
  • Am I helping them understand the value of delayed gratification?
Reflection Synthesis Questions:
  • What are you going to do in order to build academic confidence?
  • What are you going to do in order to achieve maximum potential?
  • What are you going to do in order to prepare for future success?
Inspirational Reminders:
  • "If you lower your expectations, they will always be met"
  • "Procrastination and potential are mortal enemies"
  • "If you don't know where you're going, how you gonna get there?"
  • Steve Prefontaine: "To give anything less than your best is to sacrifice the gift"
